# Awesome Open-World Alignment

A curated resource map for evaluating AI systems beyond direct human supervision.

Use these pages when filling a card field, designing a verifier stack, or checking whether a benchmark claim is overextended. Each entry includes a link plus a one-line relevance note.

## Topic Map

- [Claim type and measurement validity](claim_type_and_measurement_validity.md): resources for stating what an evaluation result actually measures.
- [Human-signal limits](human_signal_limits.md): resources on where approval, preference, and expert labels become weak evidence.
- [Scalable oversight](scalable_oversight.md): resources on supervising systems that may exceed unaided human evaluation.
- [Verifier-stack design](verifier_stack_design.md): resources on layered, independent evidence channels.
- [Hard constraints](hard_constraints.md): resources for constraints that should not be traded away for score.
- [Absent stakeholders](absent_stakeholders.md): resources on documentation, representation, and sociotechnical blind spots.
- [Monitoring and rollback](monitoring_and_rollback.md): resources for staged release, incident response, and post-deployment evidence.
- [Governance and auditing](governance_and_auditing.md): resources for audit vocabularies and risk-management mappings.
- [Sustainability and externalities](sustainability_and_externalities.md): resources for resource, climate, and rebound-effect accounting.
- [Agent evaluations](agent_evaluations.md): resources for tool-using, coding, and web agents.
- [Benchmark validity](benchmark_validity.md): resources on contamination, construct validity, and leaderboard limits.
- [Uncertainty and abstention](uncertainty_and_abstention.md): resources for calibration, unknowns, and escalation conditions.

## Contribution Standard

When adding a resource, include a stable link and one sentence explaining which Evaluation Card field it strengthens. Avoid generic AI safety links that do not improve evaluation, verification, monitoring, or governance practice.
