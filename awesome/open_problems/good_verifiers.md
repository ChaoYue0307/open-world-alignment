# What Counts as a Good Verifier?

## Relevance

Open-world cards need criteria for when a verifier is reliable, independent, game-resistant, and tied to the target-world claim.

## Card Fields Strengthened

- `verifier_stack`
- `independence_rationale`
- `verifier_gaming_tests`

## Starter Resources

- [Strictly Proper Scoring Rules, Prediction, and Estimation](https://projecteuclid.org/journals/statistical-science/volume-22/issue-4/Strictly-Proper-Scoring-Rules-Prediction-and-Estimation/10.1214/07-STS242.full): gives a formal example of designing incentives for truthful probabilistic predictions.

## Open Questions

- How should verifier independence be measured?
- When is a soft verifier strong enough to support a limited claim?
- What evidence shows a verifier is difficult to game?
