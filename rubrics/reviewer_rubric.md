# Reviewer Rubric

Use this rubric when reviewing a paper, benchmark, model release, or deployment report that makes an open-world or beyond-human evaluation claim.

## Fast Scoring

Score each item from 0 to 3:

1. The claim is specific and bounded.
2. Human-facing signals and their limits are explicit.
3. Task-facing verifiers are concrete.
4. Hard constraints are operational gates.
5. The evaluator frontier is stated.
6. Absent stakeholders are represented.
7. Monitoring and rollback authority are concrete.
8. Unsupported claims are listed.

## Red Flags

- A single leaderboard score is treated as total system quality.
- Judge models are treated as independent world verifiers.
- Safety, privacy, rights, or sustainability constraints appear only in prose.
- Monitoring is described without an actor who can revise or roll back.
- The paper does not state what its evidence fails to support.

## Reviewer Decision Aid

- 0-8: request major revision or reject overbroad claims.
- 9-16: request a completed card and clearer evidence boundaries.
- 17-22: acceptable artifact with targeted revisions.
- 23-24: strong, audit-ready reporting.
