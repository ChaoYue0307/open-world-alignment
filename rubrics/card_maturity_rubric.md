# Card Maturity Rubric

Use this rubric to score how useful an Open-World Evaluation Card is for research review, benchmark documentation, deployment review, or audit.

Each dimension is scored from 0 to 3:

- 0: missing.
- 1: mentioned but vague.
- 2: concrete but incomplete.
- 3: operational, auditable, and linked to evidence.

## Dimensions

| Dimension | What to Score |
| --- | --- |
| Claim specificity | Whether the claim is scoped and falsifiable. |
| Human-signal limitation clarity | Whether human signals and their limits are explicit. |
| Verifier-stack strength | Whether hard and soft verifiers test the target claim. |
| Verifier independence | Whether verifiers catch different failure modes. |
| Hard-constraint operationalization | Whether non-negotiables are enforceable gates. |
| Evaluator-frontier clarity | Whether abstention and escalation conditions are clear. |
| Absent-stakeholder coverage | Whether affected non-users and downstream stakeholders are represented. |
| Monitoring and rollback concreteness | Whether monitoring signals and rollback authority are mechanical. |
| Evidence reproducibility | Whether artifacts, datasets, harnesses, and notes support review. |

## Interpretation

- 0-8: structurally weak.
- 9-15: usable draft.
- 16-22: evidence-aware.
- 23-27: audit-ready.

The score is not a safety certification. It is a maturity signal for evaluation documentation.
