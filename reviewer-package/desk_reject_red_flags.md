# Desk-Reject Red Flags

These are severe artifact-quality problems for papers making broad open-world, safety, deployment, or beyond-human claims.

- No card, checklist, or equivalent claim-boundary artifact is provided.
- The central claim cannot be mapped to a target-world outcome.
- The evidence is only a benchmark score and the paper claims general safety or alignment.
- Human approval is treated as sufficient even where humans are weak supervisors.
- The paper states constraints but permits score gains to override them.
- No rollback, withdrawal, or revision process is described for deployment-stage claims.
- The paper hides affected stakeholders or externalities that are material to the claim.

Desk rejection should be reserved for cases where the paper's claimed contribution depends on these unsupported claims rather than on a narrower technical result.
